## Understanding the Game

LinkedIn Pinpoint is a daily word puzzle game where players must guess a hidden word based on category clues and letter hints. Each day brings a new challenge that tests your vocabulary and deductive reasoning skills.

### How It Works
- A category clue is provided to narrow the word space
- Players guess letters one at a time
- Correct guesses reveal letter positions
- The goal is to guess the word with minimal attempts

## Core Strategies

### 1. Start with the Category
The category clue is your most powerful tool. If the category is "Business Terms," immediately think of words like "strategy," "revenue," "stakeholder," etc.

### 2. Vowel-First Approach
Guess common vowels (E, A, O, I) early. Most English words contain at least 2-3 vowels, and identifying them quickly narrows possibilities dramatically.

### 3. Common Letter Pairs
Look for common letter combinations:
- **TH**, **HE**, **IN**, **ER**, **AN** (most common bigrams)
- **-TION**, **-MENT**, **-NESS** (common suffixes)
- **RE-**, **UN-**, **PRE-** (common prefixes)

### 4. Word Length Analysis
The number of blanks tells you the word length, which immediately eliminates many possibilities.

## Advanced Techniques

### Pattern Recognition
After revealing a few letters, look for patterns:
- If you see `_ _ E E _`, think of words with double E (e.g., "greed", "steep")
- If you see `_ R _ _ T _ _ N`, consider words ending in -TION

### Process of Elimination
Track which letters you've guessed. Each wrong guess eliminates entire word families.

### Contextual Guessing
LinkedIn Pinpoint often uses professional/business vocabulary. When stuck, think about:
- Corporate terminology
- Industry-specific jargon
- Professional skills and competencies

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Ignoring the category** - Always start with the category clue
2. **Guessing rare letters first** - Save Q, X, Z, J for last
3. **Not tracking eliminated letters** - Keep a mental or physical list
4. **Overthinking** - The answer is usually a common professional term
5. **Giving up too early** - Use all available hints before seeking help
